Sri Aurobindo's principal philosophical work, articulating the metaphysics of consciousness-as-substance, involution and evolution, and the nature of Sat-Chit-Ananda (Existence-Consciousness-Bliss).

Key passages cited in this vault:

- ["Unless mind was inherent in brute Matter"](https://incarnateword.in/cwsa/12/nature?search=Unless%20mind%20was%20inherent%20in%20brute#p1) — intelligence cannot arise from matter void of it. (Ch: "Nature")
- ["spirit secret even in things"](https://incarnateword.in/cwsa/12/man-and-superman?search=spirit%20secret%20even%20in%20things#p89) — matter is not void of consciousness.
- ["intelligence there, asleep, involved, latent"](https://incarnateword.in/cwsa/12/man-and-superman?search=intelligence%20there,%20asleep,%20involved,%20latent#p51) — intelligence awaiting conditions for emergence. (Ch: "Man and the Supermind")
- ["anterior to evolution"](https://incarnateword.in/cwsa/12/nature?search=anterior%20to%20evolution.#p10) — a part remains aware below the surface mind. (Ch: "Nature")
- ["one thing-in-itself seen diversely"](https://incarnateword.in/cwsa/12/sachchidananda?search=one%20thing-in-itself%20seen%20diversely;#p2) — Sat-Chit-Ananda as a single reality. (Ch: "Sachchidananda")
- ["self-aware and therefore all-aware"](https://incarnateword.in/cwsa/12/man-and-superman?search=that%20is%20self-aware%20and%20therefore%20all-aware.#p82) — consciousness as inherently self-knowing.
